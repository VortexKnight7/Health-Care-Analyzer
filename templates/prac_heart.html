HEART DISEASES

Heart diseases, also known as cardiovascular diseases, are a group of medical conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. These conditions can lead to various problems with the heart's structure or function, hindering its ability to pump blood effectively and supply the body with oxygen and nutrients. Heart diseases can range from mild and manageable conditions to severe and life-threatening situations

Types of Heart diseases

  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD):

    • CAD is the most prevalent type of heart disease. It occurs when the coronary arteries, which supply oxygen-rich blood to the heart muscle, become narrowed or blocked due to the buildup of fatty deposits called plaque. This narrowing reduces blood flow to the heart, leading to chest pain or discomfort known as angina. If a plaque ruptures and causes a complete blockage of blood flow, it can result in a heart attack.

  • Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction):

    • A heart attack occurs when there is a sudden, complete blockage of blood flow to a part of the heart muscle, usually due to the formation of a blood clot on a plaque-damaged artery. The lack of blood supply leads to the death of that portion of the heart muscle, which can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

  • Heart Failure:

    • Heart failure is a condition in which the heart becomes weakened and unable to pump blood effectively. It can happen gradually over time, often as a result of long-term conditions such as CAD, hypertension, or heart valve problems. Heart failure means that the heart cannot meet the body's demands for blood and oxygen, leading to symptoms like shortness of breath, fatigue, and fluid retention.

  • Heart Valve Disease:

    • This condition involves problems with the heart's valves, which are responsible for ensuring that blood flows in the right direction through the heart chambers. Valvular diseases can include valve stenosis (narrowing) or valve regurgitation (leaking).

  • Congenital Heart Defects:

    • These are heart abnormalities that are present at birth. They can range from simple conditions with little impact on health to complex defects that require immediate medical attention.
